notes on qua+

qua

  • adj plus noun - which (can be person or thing)     qua viro   qua membri
  • adj with no noun - who, usually    which one (can be person or thing, using context)
  • appears to be just like kiu

qui

  • (plural n) - plural of qua - who (plural), which (things, facts)
  • with noun - todo
  • with no noun - which ones
  • which ones (plural of qua)
  • appears to be just like kiuj
  • there is also sth about appearing as a plural of qua with no noun

quo

  • (n) - what thing
  • quo eventis?  what happened?
  • appears to be just like kio

ta

  • adj plus noun - that (can be person or thing)    ta soldato   ta kavalo    ta fosi   ta libri
  • adj with no noun - that one (can be person or thing)     ta esas
  • appears to be just like tiu

ti

  • (plural n) - plural of ta - those things, those (things, facts)
  • with noun - todo
  • with no noun - those ones
  • those ones (plural of ta)
  • appears to be just like tiuj
  • there is also sth about appearing as a plural of ta with no noun

to

  • (n) - that thing (thing, fact)
  • to eventis.  that happened.
  • appears to be just like tio


ula

  • adj plus noun - some (can be person or thing)    ula libro
  • can it ever be this??  adj with no noun - some one (can be person or thing)   or is that for ulu?
  • appears to be part of iu

ulu

  • hmmm, not available for qua and ta!?
  • not an adjective??  means no noun ever?  never: plus noun ??
  • with no noun - someone, somebody (can it ever be a nonperson??)
  • appears to be part of iu

uli

  • (plural n) - plural of ula - some (persons or things)
  • with noun - todo
  • with no noun - some ones
  • appears to be just like iuj
  • is there anything about appearing as a plural of ula with no noun?

ulo

  • (n) - some thing (thing, fact)
  • ulo eventis.  something happened.
  • appears to be just like io
