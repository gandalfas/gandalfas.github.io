Teacher's Research

how to translate:  I am well. -> "Mi estas bona." ?   or  "Mi estas bone." ?

Neither!  To translate the real meaning of this is:   Mi fartas bone.

Mi estas bona - I am good. (not evil).
Mi estas bone - this is incorrect Esperanto - esti is a special kind of word, and you can't use it like fartas - it does not take adverbs.

how to say store?

vendejo - store, shop, market

  • vendi - to sell, whether at a building or anywhere
  • vendisto - shop seller or door-to-door seller (no building)

butiko - shop, store

  • butikisto - shop seller

magazeno - big store, butikego, warehouse

ke

ke - that

ke is a conjunction - it connects two clauses (sentences within a sentence).
It does not mean the same as tiu.

Mi diras ke vi estas mia amiko. - I say that you are my friend.
Ŝi opinias ke la viro amas ŝin. - She thinks that the man loves her.

In English, sometimes we leave out that.   (try it)
In Esperanto we never leave out ke.

Adjectives - the Comparative and Superlative

With adjectives, we can compare two things (comparative) or we can compare many things (superlative).

In English, there are three ways.

-er, -est   -- many short, common words work this way

Jack is happy.    I am happier than him.  (two)       Bob is the happiest guy I know.  (many)
Bill is fast.         Glen is faster than Bill.  (two)      The man from Kenya is the fastest of them all.   (many)

more, most   -- longer words tend to use this method.  we don't use the above with these.

Lily is beautiful.        Sophie is more beautiful than Lily.         Jill is the most beautiful woman in Pittsburgh.
The boy is practical.  That girl is more practical than him.       Albert is the most practical in the class.

irregular   -- what can we say?  it's English

She is good.       He is better.      Albert is the best.
He is bad.           Josh is worse.   Gloria is the worst.

In Esperanto, there is one way.

Jack estas feliĉa.     Mi estas pli feliĉa ol lin.            Bob estas la plej feliĉa viro el ĉiuj.
Lily estas bela.        Sophie estas pli bela ol Lily.     Jill estas la plej bela virino en Pitsburgo.
Ŝi estas bona.          Li estas pli bona.                        Albert estas la plej bona.

ol is used with pli to mean than.
el is used with plej to mean out of.

Adverbs - the Comparative and Superlative

These work the same as adjectives.

Fifi marŝas kviete.     Fido marŝas pli kviete ol Fifi.     Louie marŝas la plej kviete el ĉiuj.

ĉu ne

ĉu ne - don't you?  isn't it?  didn't they?  isn't it so?

ĉu ne is added to the end of a sentence to make it into a question.

Vi kantas, ĉu ne? - You sing, don't you?
Li estas la plej bona, ĉu ne?  -  He is the best, isn't he?
Ili kuris al la butiko, ĉu ne?  -  They ran to the store, didn't they?
Ĝi manĝis la torton, ĉu ne?  -  It ate the pie, didn't it?

Johnny Kaiser:   He will drive the Ford, not?
