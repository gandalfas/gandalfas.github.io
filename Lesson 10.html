adverbs

review:
An adjective modifies (describes) a noun.
Adjectives answer the questions:  which one?   what kind of?   how many?

the muddy dog                (which dog?)
a steady hand                  (what kind of hand?)
five cats                           (how many cats?)

An adverb modifies (describes) a verb.
Adverbs answer the questions:   how?  when?  where?
We often use -ly to mark adverbs.

I marched steadily to the fort.                (how did you march?)
I swam rapidly away from the shark.     (how did you swim?)

I walked to school yesterday.                 (when did you walk?)

Please sit here.                                        (where should I sit?)
They looked everywhere.                       (where did they look?)

An adverb can also modify (describe) an adjective.

That is an incredibly blue coat.               (how blue?)

there is no standard ending, but -aŭ is common.

apenaŭ - barely        (how was it done?)
tre - very                   (how bright is it?)

hodiaŭ - today          (when are you going?)
hieraŭ - yesterday
morgaŭ - tomorrow
nun - now

tie - there                  (where are you going?)


adverbs from adjectives

an adverb can be derived from an adjective.

for this kind, there is a standard ending: -e

Rule:  remove -a, then add -e to the root.

bela - beautiful
bele - beautifully    (how was it done?)

feliĉa - happy
feliĉe - happily        (how would you do it?)

forta - strong
forte - strongly        (how do you feel about it?)

The -ano suffix

-an- - member of

komitato - committee
komitatano - a committee-member

urbo - city
urbano - member of city, citizen
